\section*{6.1 Lucas theorem}

For non-negative integers $m$ and $n$ and a prime $p$, the following congruence relation holds:

$$
\binom{m}{n} \equiv \prod_{i=0}^{k} \binom{m_i}{n_i} \pmod{p},
$$

where:

$$
m = m_k p^k + m_{k-1} p^{k-1} + \dots + m_1 p + m_0,
$$

and:

$$
n = n_k p^k + n_{k-1} p^{k-1} + \dots + n_1 p + n_0
$$

are the base $p$ expansions of $m$ and $n$ respectively. This uses the convention that $\binom{m}{n} = 0$ if $m \le n$.
